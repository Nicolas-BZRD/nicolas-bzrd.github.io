About Me - NLP Research & Large Language Models

Hey! I’m Nicolas Boizard, a 2nd year PhD student at CentraleSupélec (Université Paris-Saclay), supervised by Pierre Colombo and working on applied NLP.

My research focuses on large-scale training of encoder and decoder models, including multilingual ones like EuroBERT, EuroLLM, and CroissantLLM. Since large-scale training is costly, I’m also developing efficient techniques by analyzing objectives and using knowledge distillation, leading to the Universal Logit Distillation Loss, which enables distillation across model families.

My work has led to invited talks at Meta, Cohere, and several academic institutions. My PhD is funded through the CIFRE program in collaboration with Diabolocom, where I also advise R&D efforts on various LLM-related projects.

Feel free to reach out to connect, collaborate, or discuss potential invited talks!
